The Symptoms of Brain Diseases

When the brain is healthy, it works quickly and automatically. But when you have a brain disease, it may affect how well you can function and do your daily activities. Some common brain diseases include: Brain tumors, Degenerative nerve diseases, Encephalitis (inflammation in the brain), Genetic brain disorders, Strokes, Traumatic brain injuries (TBIs).
The symptoms of brain diseases vary widely, depending on the specific problem. In some cases, damage is permanent. In other cases, treatments such as surgery, medicines, or therapies such as physical, occupational, and speech therapies, may cure the disease or improve the symptoms.

Conditions & Treatments

There are many conditions of the brain and spine which can be treated through neurointerventional procedures, some of the most well-known including aneurysms, strokes and spinal compression fractures. Although neurointervention is not the appropriate treatment for every individual patient case that falls within these categories, these surgical techniques have proven themselves as viable and effective therapies that offer patients many benefits over traditional treatments, such as shorter recovery times, fewer complications and less post-operative pain.

Maintaining brain health

Reducing Risk of Brain Diseases

Maintaining brain health and reducing the risk of brain diseases involves a balanced lifestyle that includes a nutritious diet rich in brain-boosting foods, regular physical and mental exercise, and adequate sleep. Managing stress through relaxation techniques and staying socially active are also crucial. Avoiding harmful substances like excessive alcohol, smoking, and drugs, along with regular medical check-ups, can help in early detection and prevention. By making these healthy choices, you can protect your brain and enhance cognitive function throughout your life.
